## THE TWO CHANNELS — HONEST PICTURE

### Channel 1 — B2B (Sub-Agents) — 70% of current business

Sub-agents are independent referral partners — individuals or small agencies
who send student leads to DeAssists. When a lead converts, the agent earns
a commission (percentage or flat fee per student).

This is the current engine of the business. Without sub-agents, volume drops
significantly. The portal CRM is being built in part to manage this channel
properly — tracking which agent sent which lead, which converted, and what
commission is owed.

**Current problem:** Commission tracking, attribution, and agent communication
all happen manually (WhatsApp + Google Sheets). This is the primary operational
risk — it does not scale.

**Fix being built:** Portal lead management with agent attribution, commission
tracking, and status automation.

### Channel 2 — B2C (Direct) — 30% of current business

Direct students who find DeAssists via website, Instagram, WhatsApp, or
referral from a previous student. DeAssists keeps 100% of the service fee.

**Why this matters:** Every B2C conversion is higher margin. The strategic goal
is to grow this channel — using social media, the public website, and content
— so DeAssists is not dependent on agents for the majority of revenue.

**What enables B2C growth:** A connected CRM so the small team can handle more
leads without dropping quality. This is why the portal build is the first
priority before any team expansion.

## KNOWN GAPS — WHAT VEERABHADRA AND PORTAL MUST FIX

1. **Agent tracking is manual** — WhatsApp + Sheets. No attribution, no
   commission record, no performance visibility.
   Fix: Portal agent module with lead source tagging and commission calculation.

2. **Shon is single point of failure** — critical knowledge is not documented.
   Fix: Service SOPs per scenario, brain files, VEERABHADRA sessions.

3. **Two-tracker dependency** — 369 Tracker + BCBT sheet = overhead and errors.
   Fix: Unified portal with BCBT import (Phase 6 migration).

4. **B2C is 30% and under-resourced** — no automated lead nurturing, no
   structured follow-up system.
   Fix: CRM with lead stages, follow-up workflows, and eventually automation.

5. **Germany team is intern-level** — Sajir and Amala need structured SOPs.
   Fix: Service SOPs for FSJ, Ausbildung, Accommodation via VEERABHADRA.

6. **Fee confirmation is manual** — confirmation happens via WhatsApp with no log.
   Fix: Fee confirmation log inside portal with date, rep, confirmed fee, validity.

7. **Social leads enter manually via Gopika** — lag and no automation.
   Fix: WATI → portal direct integration (future automation layer).
